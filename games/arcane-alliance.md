## **Arcane Alliance**

---

### **Update March 2026**

- 5 single color decks
- 2 teams of 4 players
- no player elimination
- win condition is score-based
- deck drafting order: team 1, team 2, team 2, team 1

- 60 cards (40 are decided by meta-app)
- each player can bring their own 20 cards (non-lands) into the chosen deck.

### **Update December 2024**

**Game Mode Title:** Håkon the Oathbreaker: The Arcane Alliance

**Overview:**
This game mode is a team-based, multiplayer format for Magic: The Gathering designed for a minimum of four players divided into two teams. It emphasizes teamwork, strategic planning, and incorporates a unique "Arcane Spin" mechanic that adds an element of unpredictability without causing single-player elimination.

---

**Objective:**
Each team aims to reduce the opposing team's shared life total to zero or meet specific victory conditions first, all while navigating the challenges and opportunities presented by the Arcane Spin.

---

**Teams:**
- **Team Håkon**
- **Team Arcane Spin Alliance**

---

**Setup:**
1. **Deck Construction:**
   - **Format:** Players build decks following the Oathbreaker format rules.
     - **Deck Size:** 60 cards, including your Oathbreaker and Signature Spell.
     - **Oathbreaker:** Each player's deck is led by a planeswalker (their Oathbreaker).
     - **Signature Spell:** A sorcery or instant card that matches the Oathbreaker's color identity.
   - **Theme Integration:** Players on Team Håkon are encouraged to use graveyard mechanics, while Team Arcane Spin Alliance focuses on spells and abilities that manipulate chance or randomness.

2. **Life Totals:**
   - **Shared Life Total:** Each team starts with a collective life total of **60**.

3. **Starting the Game:**
   - Determine which team goes first through a coin toss or random method.
   - Players draw their opening hands as usual.

---

**Special Rules:**

1. **Arcane Spin Mechanic:**
   - At the beginning of each team's turn, that team spins the **Arcane Wheel** or draws from the **Arcane Deck**.
   - **Arcane Wheel/Deck Effects:** These are predefined random effects that can influence the game in various ways. Examples include:
     - **Mana Surge:** All team members add one mana of any color to their mana pools.
     - **Grave Exchange:** Each player may return a creature from their graveyard to their hand.
     - **Arcane Swap:** Teams exchange control of a nonland permanent of their choice.
     - **Mystic Shield:** The team gains hexproof until their next turn.
   - The effects are designed to be balanced and affect both teams equally when possible.

2. **No Player Elimination:**
   - Players remain in the game even if their individual resources are depleted.
   - If a player would be eliminated (e.g., forced to draw from an empty library), they instead:
     - **Library Recycle:** Shuffle their graveyard into their library and draw a new hand of three cards.
   - This ensures continuous participation from all players.

3. **Team Turns:**
   - Teams take turns collectively.
   - **Sequence of Play:**
     - **Upkeep Phase:** Resolve any upkeep triggers, including the Arcane Spin.
     - **Main Phase:** Players discuss and play their cards individually but can coordinate strategies.
     - **Combat Phase:** Team members can attack collectively, choosing how their creatures attack the opposing team.
     - **End Phase:** Resolve end-of-turn effects.

4. **Communication:**
   - Open communication is encouraged within teams for strategizing.
   - Private communication (like passing notes) is allowed but should be agreed upon before the game starts.

5. **Graveyard Casting (Team Håkon Exclusive):**
   - Players on Team Håkon may cast creature cards from their graveyards as though they were in their hands.
   - Such creatures enter the battlefield with a **"Necrotic Counter"** and gain "If this creature would leave the battlefield, exile it instead."

6. **Arcane Manipulation (Team Arcane Spin Alliance Exclusive):**
   - Players on Team Arcane Spin Alliance may manipulate the Arcane Spin by:
     - **Once per Game:** Choosing to re-spin the Arcane Wheel or draw a second card from the Arcane Deck and selecting which effect to apply.
     - **Spellcasting Bonus:** Spells with randomized effects (like coin flips) have doubled outcomes (e.g., flip two coins instead of one).

---

**Victory Conditions:**
- **Primary Victory:** Reduce the opposing team's shared life total to zero.
- **Alternate Victory:** Achieve a team-specific goal, such as accumulating a set number of **Arcane Points**.
  - **Earning Arcane Points:**
    - Successfully manipulating the Arcane Spin.
    - Resolving certain spells or abilities specified before the game starts.

---

**Additional Rules:**

1. **Balance Adjustments:**
   - Teams should have an equal number of players.
   - If teams are uneven, adjust starting life totals or Arcane Spin effects to balance the game.

2. **Deck Restrictions:**
   - Standard ban lists apply unless modified by group consensus.
   - Players should avoid infinite combos that could detract from the collaborative spirit of the game.

3. **Optional Role Assignment:**
   - Assign special roles within each team (e.g., Leader, Strategist) to enhance teamwork.

---

**Theme and Flavor Integration:**

- **Håkon the Oathbreaker:** Emphasizes resurrection and relentless forces, reflected in Team Håkon's ability to cast from the graveyard.
- **The Arcane Spin Alliance:** Represents unpredictability and mastery over chance, showcased by the Arcane Spin mechanic and manipulation abilities.

---

**Example Arcane Spin Effects:**

1. **Barrier of Wards:** Opposing team’s creatures can’t attack during their next turn.
2. **Chaotic Reversal:** Swap the graveyards of all players.
3. **Temporal Boost:** Your team takes an additional turn after this one.
4. **Mana Flare:** Until your next turn, whenever a player taps a land for mana, that player adds one mana of any type that land produced.

---

**Tips for a Successful Game:**

- **Strategize as a Team:** Encourage open discussion to plan moves and anticipate opponents.
- **Embrace the Chaos:** The Arcane Spin introduces unpredictable elements; adapt your strategies accordingly.
- **Focus on Fun:** Remember that the goal is to enjoy a collaborative and exciting game with friends.

**Rules**

- 2 TEAMS. Minimum 4 PLAYERS. Maximum 10 PLAYERS.
- The fist deck is chosen by the winner of stone-paper-scissor, which becomes Player 1 for Team 1. Deck two and three are selected by Team 2. Player 2 on Team 1 gets the last remaining deck.

- Each life consists of 32 PIECES.
- You lose the game when your team has 0 LIFE remaining.
- Each player starts the game with their TIER 1 COMMANDER on the board. The TIER 2 COMMANDER comes into play for your team after your team loses their first life.
- The game follows the standard gameplay rules of Magic the Gathering.
- CULTURE decides which team starts the game.

## **The Decks**

### 1. Celestial Codex | *White/Blue*

- **Culture:** The Celestial Codex is a society governed by divine laws and celestial observations, blending the pursuit of justice with arcane knowledge. Members value harmony, wisdom, and order, dedicating themselves to the study of celestial bodies and their influence on moral conduct. Their cities resemble vast libraries and observatories, with architecture that mirrors the constellations.

- **Warfare:** In battle, the Celestial Codex employs a mix of tactical spellcasting and strategic maneuvers guided by their prophetic insights. They rely on controlling the battlefield through enchantments that limit enemy movements and counteracts, alongside celestial beings summoned to their aid.

- **Guilds:**

    - Etheric Summit
    - Seraphic Syndicate

### 2. Shadow Nexus | *Blue/Black*

- **Culture:** This enigmatic guild operates from the shadows, valuing secrecy, intelligence, and manipulation. The Shadow Nexus is a network of spies, assassins, and mages who deal in information, leveraging it to maintain a balance of power or to tilt it in their favor. Their society is built around subterfuge, with their architecture mirroring the labyrinthine complexity of their operations.

- **Warfare:** Warfare for the Shadow Nexus is a blend of psychological tactics and guerrilla warfare. They prefer to strike from the shadows, utilizing illusions, mind control, and poison to dismantle their opponents from within. Their warriors are trained in the art of stealth and are experts in the use of magical artifacts designed for espionage.

- **Guilds:**

    - Nocturnal Covenant
    - Veilbound Enclave

---

### 3. Ember Cult | *Black/Red*

- **Culture:** The Ember Cult celebrates freedom, passion, and chaos. Its followers are entertainers, artists, and thrill-seekers who worship through performance, where fire and blood are common themes. Their society is nomadic, moving from one grand spectacle to another, leaving stories of awe and horror in their wake.

- **Warfare:** The Ember Cult's approach to warfare is as chaotic as their lifestyle. They employ shock tactics, reveling in close-quarters combat where they can unleash their ferocity. Their warriors use fire and blood magic to demoralize and dismantle their foes, turning the battlefield into a frenzied spectacle of power.

- **Guilds:**

    - Crimson Cabal
    - Sanguine Sect

---

### 4. Wildheart Tribe | *Red/Green*

- **Culture:** Rooted in nature, the Wildheart Tribe lives in a state of symbiosis with the wilds, embracing the primal essence of life. They value strength, freedom, and growth, living in tight-knit communities that roam the forests. Their structures are built from the living environment around them, ensuring minimal disruption to nature.

- **Warfare:** The Wildheart Tribe excels in guerrilla warfare, using the terrain to their advantage. Their combat style is ferocious and unpredictable, with beasts and plant magic augmenting their physical prowess. They are masters of ambush, using the land itself as both weapon and shield.

- **Guilds:**

    - Feral Conclave
    - Primal Brigade

---

### 5. Harmony Collective | *Green/White*

- **Culture:** The Harmony Collective is founded on the principles of unity, peace, and cooperation. Members work together in communal living, blending their society seamlessly with nature. Theirs is a deeply spiritual culture, where music, healing, and agriculture are as much a part of their identity as their pacifist beliefs.

- **Warfare:** When forced to defend themselves, the Harmony Collective relies on defensive tactics and the mobilization of the collective's will. They use healing magics and summon nature's guardians to protect their people, preferring to subdue rather than destroy their attackers.

- **Guilds:**

    - Verdant Union
    - Sylvan Fellowship

---

### 6. Spectral Consortium | *White/Black*

- **Culture:** The Spectral Consortium operates like a theocratic mafia, where wealth, power, and spirituality intersect. Members are part of a strict hierarchy, with the upper echelons often comprised of powerful spirits and the living bound to them through debt or devotion. Their society is built on the belief that wealth can buy anything, including eternity, with grand cathedrals and mausoleums that stand as both places of worship and power.

- **Warfare:** The Spectral Consortium wages a war of attrition and manipulation, using both economic sanctions and spiritual warfare. They are known to deploy accursed spirits to haunt their enemies, drain foes' vitality, and employ necromancy to raise fallen adversaries as indebted soldiers.

- **Guilds:**

    - Ethereal Dominion
    - Phantom League

---

### 7. Decay Alliance | *Black/Green*

- **Culture:** Embracing the cycle of life and death, the Decay Alliance thrives in the underbelly of decay, finding beauty and strength in rot and renewal. They are a resilient and adaptable society, living in harmony with fungi and vermin, recycling the dead to nourish the living. Their architecture is woven into the catacombs and ruins, creating ecosystems that sustain their way of life.

- **Warfare:** In battle, the Decay Alliance employs swarms of insects, plagues, and undead warriors. They use guerrilla tactics, striking from below or using the environment to unleash deadly pathogens on their enemies. Their warriors are unyielding, bolstered by necromantic energies that allow them to fight even in death.

- **Guilds:**

    - Rotborn Guild
    - Undergrowth Unity

---

### 8. Biomystic Syndicate | *Green/Blue*

- **Culture:** The Biomystic Syndicate is dedicated to the evolution and enhancement of life through magical and genetic manipulation. They value innovation, knowledge, and the potential of life’s adaptability. Their society is a blend of laboratory and sanctuary, where diverse life forms coexist and are studied. Their structures are alive, grown and adapted to suit their needs, reflecting their belief in the potential for life to evolve beyond its natural boundaries.

- **Warfare:** The Biomystic Syndicate’s approach to warfare involves the use of bioengineered creatures and mutagenic magics. They adapt their organisms for various tactical roles, creating symbiotic battle units that can heal, adapt, or transform according to the needs of the battlefield. Their warriors are themselves experiments, capable of adapting their bodies for combat efficiency.

- **Guilds:**

    - Genesis Pool
    - Evolunion

---

### 9. Stormbinders | *Blue/Red*

- **Culture:** The Stormbinders are a guild of inventors, scientists, and mages obsessed with the harnessing of elemental energies, primarily focusing on lightning and fire. They value creativity, discovery, and the explosive beauty of unbridled energy. Their society is chaotic and ever-changing, with laboratories and workshops scattered like a web of innovation. Their architecture is eclectic, often serving as testaments to the power and danger of their experiments.

- **Warfare:** Warfare for the Stormbinders is a spectacle of destruction and creativity, utilizing volatile energies and experimental weaponry. They rely on shock and awe tactics, employing constructs, explosives, and elemental spells that can alter the battleground in an instant. Their warriors are as unpredictable as they are dangerous, often turning the tide of battle with a single, well-timed experiment.

- **Guilds:**

    - Aetherflux Assembly
    - Arcanospark Collective

---

### 10. Valorforge Legion | *Red/White*

- **Culture:** The Valorforge Legion is built on the principles of honor, justice, and the collective good. They are a militant society, organized and disciplined, where every citizen is a soldier dedicated to the protection of their ideals. Their cities are fortified, majestic, and strategic, designed to reflect their strength and resilience. 

- **Warfare:** The Valorforge Legion engages in warfare with precision, strategy, and unwavering courage. They excel in organized combat, utilizing well-trained armies that operate in perfect harmony. Their tactics involve a blend of magical and martial prowess, with a focus on defensive formations and calculated strikes designed to disarm and disable without unnecessary slaughter.

- **Guilds:**

    - Dawnblade Battalion
    - Warlight Vanguard
